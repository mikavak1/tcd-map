Provost's House

Primary Function: Official Residence of the Provost / Administrative Hub

Hours: Private Residence / Limited Public Access

Contact: Provost's Office +353 1 896 4362

General Information

Built in 1759, the Provost's House is a Palladian masterpiece and one of the most important historic houses in Ireland. It remains the official private residence of the Provost of Trinity College Dublin and serves as a venue for significant university events and hosting international guests.

Accessibility Highlights

While the building is a historic 18th-century residence, step-free access is available for official visitors via the side gate on Grafton Street. The ground-floor reception rooms and the private garden are accessible. Due to the heritage nature of the site, access to upper floors is restricted to stairs only.

Facilities & Student Services

As a private residence, this building does not house general student facilities. However, it is the primary administrative base for the Provost's Office and contains some of the university's most prestigious art collections.
