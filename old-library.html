Old Library (The Long Room)

Departments: Library Services & Visitor Experience

Hours: Mon-Sun 09:30 - 17:00

Contact: Visitor Desk +353 1 896 1127

General Information

Built between 1712 and 1732, the Old Library is home to the Book of Kells and the magnificent Long Room. This 65-meter hall houses 200,000 of the library’s oldest books and remains one of the most impressive libraries in the world.

Accessibility Highlights

Step-free access is fully provided. Visitors can use the lift to reach the Book of Kells exhibition and the Long Room. Staff are always on hand to assist with navigation.

What to See

Don't miss the 15th-century Brian Boru Harp and the 1916 Proclamation of the Irish Republic. The Long Room also features a series of marble busts representing the great thinkers of the Western world.
