Public Theatre (Exam Hall)

Departments: Academic Registry / Graduation Ceremonies

Hours: Mon–Fri 09:00 – 17:00 (Event Dependent)

Contact: Reception +353 1 896 4500

General Information

The Public Theatre, widely known as the Exam Hall, was designed by Sir William Chambers and completed in 1791. This neoclassical masterpiece is the traditional venue for University examinations, commencements (graduation ceremonies), and significant public lectures. It sits opposite the Chapel, forming the architectural 'twins' of Front Square.

Accessibility Highlights

The ground floor is physically accessible during scheduled events and ceremonies. A temporary ramp can be provided for the main entrance upon request. The internal seating area is on a level plane, though the historic nature of the building means upper galleries are accessible only via stairs.

Facilities & Student Services

As a core ceremonial hall, it features a magnificent historic organ and portraits of significant university figures. During exam seasons, it provides a silent, formal environment for students.
