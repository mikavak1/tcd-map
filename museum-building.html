Museum Building

Departments: Geology, Geography and Civil Engineering

Hours: Mon–Fri 09:00 – 17:00

Contact: Reception +353 1 896 1074

General Information

Built between 1853 and 1857, the Museum Building is one of the most celebrated examples of Venetian Gothic architecture in Ireland. Designed by Deane and Woodward, the building is famous for its intricate stone carvings of animals and plants by the O'Shea brothers. It houses the Department of Geology and its impressive Geological Museum.

Accessibility Highlights

The building has a ramped accessible entrance located at the East side of the building (facing the Berkeley Library). Inside, a passenger lift serves all floors, including the main hall and the upper lecture theatres. Accessible toilets are available on the ground floor. The central hall features iconic Irish marble pillars from all over the country.

Facilities & Student Services

This building contains several specialized teaching laboratories, the Geology Museum, and the main lecture theatre for the School of Natural Sciences. Most heavy-duty automated doors are installed in high-traffic corridors to assist with navigation.
